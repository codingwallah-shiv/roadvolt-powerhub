About the Project

Millions of footsteps and vehicle movements happen every day, and nearly all of that mechanical energy is wasted. Most renewable energy solutions need large-scale infrastructure and high installation costs — RoadVolt + PowerHub is a scalable proof-of-concept that captures this everyday energy instead, and secures it with smart access control.

Problem

Wasted energy, everywhere

Footsteps and vehicle movement generate mechanical energy that simply disappears — no infrastructure currently captures it at small scale.

Approach

Harvest, then protect it

RoadVolt captures and converts the energy; PowerHub stores it and only releases it to an authorized RFID-verified user.

Goal

A complete ecosystem

Not just generation — a full cycle of harvest, convert, store, secure, and access, demonstrated in one working prototype.

Module 01

RoadVolt

A compact energy harvesting platform that converts mechanical pressure — from footsteps or toy vehicles — into electrical energy using piezoelectric discs.

Mechanical structure

01 · Top PlateMDF pressure plate receives the applied force
02 · SpringsCompression springs transfer that force downward
03 · Rubber Sheet3mm natural rubber sheet cushions and distributes pressure
04 · Piezo ArrayPiezoelectric disc array generates AC voltage
05 · Base PlateMDF base board (300 × 300mm) anchors the whole platform

Working principle

  1. Pressure is applied on the platform
  2. Springs compress and transfer force
  3. Piezoelectric discs generate AC voltage
  4. Bridge rectifier converts AC into DC
  5. Capacitor smooths the output
  6. Energy is transferred to PowerHub

Components used

  • MDF base board (300 × 300mm)
  • MDF pressure plate
  • Piezoelectric discs
  • Soft compression springs
  • 3mm natural rubber sheet
  • Bridge rectifier module
  • 1000µF electrolytic capacitor
  • Terminal connectors
  • Hook-up wires

Module 02

PowerHub

A smart storage and access unit that stores harvested energy and provides RFID-based secure access, controlled by a Raspberry Pi Pico W.

Main features

  • RFID authentication
  • Smart locking system
  • OLED status display
  • Smart energy storage
  • Raspberry Pi Pico W controller
  • Compact 3D-printed enclosure

Working principle

  1. Harvested energy enters PowerHub
  2. Energy is stored
  3. User scans RFID card
  4. Pico W verifies authorization
  5. OLED displays system status
  6. Stepper motor unlocks the storage compartment
  7. Authorized user gains access

Components used

  • Raspberry Pi Pico W
  • RC522 RFID module
  • 0.91" OLED display (SSD1306)
  • ULN2003 stepper driver
  • 28BYJ-48 stepper motor
  • TP4056 charging module (future)
  • Rechargeable battery (future)

Why This Project Is Innovative

Unlike conventional renewable energy demonstrations that only focus on generation, RoadVolt + PowerHub demonstrates the complete ecosystem — harvest, convert, store, secure, and access — combining six disciplines into one compact educational prototype.

Renewable Energy

Piezoelectric harvesting from everyday mechanical motion.

Embedded Systems

Raspberry Pi Pico W running MicroPython as the controller.

Mechanical Engineering

Spring-and-plate pressure transfer, 3D-printed enclosure.

Electronics

Rectification, filtering, and stepper motor driving.

Security

RFID-based authentication gates physical access.

Automation

Stepper-motor lock responds automatically to verified users.
